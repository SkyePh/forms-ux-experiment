# experiment
Evaluating Step-Based vs. Single-Page Forms: Impact on User Experience in Web Applications

# Hypothesis
Does information provided in steps, help with the user experience of a web application? My prediction is that splitting the information is small chunks will make the UX easier and more smooth for the user. 

# Plan
In order to discover the answer to the question, I will create a web application which contains a form with ~15 fields (relatively large) in 2 formats. First, the user will fill the form which will provide all the fields at once stacked vertically. After they are done, they will refill the same form but this time it will be in the manner of a stepper. 
The stepper’s job is to split the information into multiple steps, therefor providing the user with limited information at each step, making the form less overwhelming. I predict that the users will prefer this format for it being less overwhelming and less cluttered. Paradoxically the users may find themselves thinking that doing 5 steps is more tedious than just filling the whole form at once which is only 1 step. Even if the 5 steps are significantly shorter and in total, the same size as the initial form, they might feel impatient having to fill 5 smaller forms rather than the whole form at the same time. This experiment will find out which format the users find better and if they find one better than the other.
To conduct this experiment, I will get the help of 10 volunteers of different technological backgrounds and ages to participate. After they complete both forms, they will complete a questionnaire I will prepare to gather the opinion of each participant.
